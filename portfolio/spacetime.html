s p a c e t i m e

for MUSIC 256A

In creating "spacetime", I first and foremost wanted to build a novel musical system. There seems to exist an unspoken assumption that drum machines should be linear in time - and understandably so, as their objective is to play catchy, danceable grooves. However, I desired a drum machine optimized for handling trippy, unpredictable rhythms, and thus came my inspiration to build a drum machine focused on manipulating time itself.

Thematically, I stuck with a literal interpretation of controlling time by designing the interface around a clock; naturally, space went hand-in-hand, so I added a galaxy for my backdrop and placed a black hole at the center. The idea then occurred to me that by manipulating time, the user is embodying a godlike being of sorts, so I chose my user-placed objects (representing each drum sound) to evoke motifs of creationism (trees, humans, and forbidden fruit in the garden of Eden), as well as cats!

The trippiness really comes into play when the user-placed objects - in their downwards spiral towards the black hole - cross a certain distance threshold. From then on, space distorts around the objects, timing is no longer linear, and the drum beat becomes unpredictable.
